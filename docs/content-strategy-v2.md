## 2. Bob's Core Thesis: Own the Disruption

### The Argument

AI is the new reengineering.

In the 1990s, ERP forced organizations to break down silos, see their entire workflow end-to-end, and fundamentally rethink how they operated. Most companies that resisted got acquired or disappeared. The ones that leaned in — even when it was painful — came out dominant.

AI is that moment again. But bigger. Because this time it's not just about visibility across silos — it's about replacing entire cost structures.

**The framework: Buy, Build, or Partner**

Every AI decision a firm makes falls into one of three buckets:
- **Buy** — Replace or optimize existing SaaS with AI-native alternatives
- **Build** — Create proprietary AI that becomes a competitive advantage
- **Partner** — Work with a team that's done it before to accelerate both

Most firms are stuck in "buy" mode — adding AI features to existing SaaS. That's table stakes. The real opportunity is in Build and Partner, where you:

1. **Replace SaaS** — Why pay $500K/year for a tool when a custom AI agent does it better for $50K to build?
2. **Optimize SaaS** — Wrap AI around existing tools to extract more value without ripping and replacing
3. **Reduce service spend** — Automate the work you're paying consultants and BPOs to do manually
4. **Reduce G&A through automation** — Back-office operations (AP, AR, scheduling, compliance, reporting) are the first domino
5. **Productize services** — Turn manual service delivery into scalable software. What your team does for 10 clients, AI can do for 1,000.

**The workforce unlock: AI doesn't replace people — it changes what they do**

This is the reengineering parallel that matters most. ERP didn't eliminate workers — it eliminated low-value tasks and freed people for judgment, relationships, and strategy. AI does the same thing, faster. The MDS coordinator who spent 45 minutes documenting now spends 5 — and uses the other 40 for clinical assessment. The RCM analyst who chased denials now prevents them. This is Bob's angle on the "AI workforce" conversation — not the generic "upskilling" narrative, but the specific operational reality of what happens when you automate the right things. (Note: DS doesn't need to own the broad AI-workforce thought leadership space — let McKinsey and the HR tech firms fight over that. Bob's version is grounded in what he's seen happen inside client operations.)

**The financial unlock: OpEx reduction funds CapEx investment**

When you cut $2M in SaaS and services spend through AI automation, that capital doesn't disappear — it funds invention. New products. New markets. Differentiation that competitors can't copy because it's built on your data, your workflows, your domain expertise.

**The DS positioning:**

Digital Scientists helps firms **own the disruption** — not just survive it. We work across the full spectrum:
- **Back office:** Automate G&A, optimize revenue cycle, reduce manual operations
- **Front office:** Build AI-powered products and experiences that differentiate
- **Invention:** Partner on net-new products, venture builds, and innovation labs

We've done it in healthcare ($10M PDPM recovery, 45→5 min documentation, 96% treat-in-place). We've done it in enterprise (Mailchimp → $12B exit). We do it in 5 days with The Experiment to prove it works before you commit.

### Content Format for LLM Citability
Every blog post should include:
