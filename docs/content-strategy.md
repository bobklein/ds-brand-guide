## 2. The Five Content Pillars

Each pillar maps to a buyer journey stage and a phase of the DS METHOD.

### Pillar 1: Pain Is Real

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Buyer stage** | Problem awareness |
| **METHOD phase** | Pre-Discover |
| **Voice** | Empathetic validation — "We see what you're dealing with" |
| **SEO targets** | healthcare operations costs, clinician burnout ROI, denied claims cost |

Validates operational pain points that healthcare leaders live with daily: documentation backlogs, denied claims, clinician burnout, disconnected systems. Names specific dollar amounts and time costs. Never prescribes a solution — just earns the right to be heard.

**Example topics:**
- Why your SNF documentation backlog costs more than you think
- The hidden revenue leak in manual MDS coding
- What clinician burnout actually costs your P&L
- What denied claims actually cost your revenue cycle team

**Maps to audit gaps:** Healthcare AI ROI measurement (copy audit gap #6), Optimize phase content (2% → target 10%+)

---

### Pillar 2: Evidence Before Investment

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Buyer stage** | Solution exploration |
| **METHOD phase** | Discover + Experiment |
| **Voice** | Analytical, rigorous — "Here's how to know before you spend" |
| **SEO targets** | healthcare AI evaluation, AI proof of concept, healthcare AI ROI |

The philosophical core of DS METHOD. Every dollar should follow evidence, not demos. Teaches readers how to evaluate, scope, and validate before committing. This is the "permission to build" pillar — readers learn the framework and can apply it independently.

**Example topics:**
- The 3-question test before any AI investment
- How to run a proof of concept that actually proves something
- Why 95% of healthcare AI projects die between pilot and production
- The proof-of-concept playbook: 30 days to validated evidence

**Maps to audit gaps:** "How to evaluate a healthcare AI vendor" (copy audit gap #1), "The true cost of a failed healthcare AI pilot" (gap #3), "What to look for in a POC partner" (gap #4)

## 4. LinkedIn Post Formats

Six rotation formats to keep the feed dynamic. Each has a defined structure and channel preference.

### Format 1: Contrarian Take

**Channel:** Bob's personal page
**Structure:** Challenge conventional wisdom in 2-3 sentences. Take a clear position. No hedging.
**Length:** 100-200 words
**Example:** "Most healthcare AI vendors don't want you to run a real POC. Because a real POC has failure criteria. And their product would fail."
**CTA:** None (engagement-driven, not traffic-driven)

### Format 2: Story Snippet

**Channel:** Bob's personal page
**Structure:** 3-paragraph narrative from a real engagement (anonymized if needed). Setup → tension → insight.
**Length:** 150-300 words
**Example:** "Last month I sat across from a CFO who had just approved his third AI vendor in two years..." → what went wrong → the insight about evaluation methodology
**CTA:** Soft ("We wrote about this" + blog link at end, not in body)

### Format 3: Data Point + Question

**Channel:** Either (Bob or DS)
**Structure:** One surprising number from a blog post, then a direct question to the audience.
**Length:** 50-100 words
**Example:** "Denied claims cost the average health system $4.9M/year in rework alone. What's your number?"
**CTA:** Blog link in first comment, not in post

### Format 4: Framework/Checklist

**Channel:** DS company page
**Structure:** Visual carousel or numbered list. 3-7 items. Actionable, not theoretical.
**Length:** Carousel (5-8 slides) or 150-250 words
**Example:** "5 questions to ask before buying healthcare AI: 1. Can you show me results with MY data?..."
**CTA:** "Full framework in the blog post" + link

### Format 5: Case Proof

**Channel:** DS company page
**Structure:** Specific result from a case study with context. Lead with the number, then explain what made it work.
**Length:** 100-200 words
**Example:** "$10M recovered in PDPM revenue. One integration. Here's what actually made it work: [3 bullet takeaways]"
**CTA:** Case study link

### Format 6: Permission Post

**Channel:** Bob's personal page
**Structure:** Empowering, generous. Gives away a framework or insight. "You don't need a vendor for this."
**Length:** 200-400 words
**Example:** "You don't need an AI vendor to evaluate your MDS documentation backlog. Here's the framework we use internally..."
**CTA:** None or soft blog link. The value is in the post itself.

## 5. Blog-to-LinkedIn Pipeline

Each blog post produces 2 LinkedIn posts (optionally 3) using the **extraction method** — not summary.

### The Process

```
1. PUBLISH BLOG (Monday/Tuesday)
   │
2. EXTRACT ONE SURPRISING POINT
   │  The single insight that would make someone stop scrolling.
   │  → LinkedIn Post 1 (Bob's page, same week)
   │
3. EXTRACT ONE ACTIONABLE FRAMEWORK
   │  A checklist, question set, or decision matrix from the blog.
   │  → LinkedIn Post 2 (DS page, same week)
   │
4. OPTIONAL: EXTRACT A CONTRARIAN TAKE
      If the blog contains a counterintuitive argument, pull it out
      as a standalone opinion post for the following week.
```

### Rules

- **LinkedIn posts never summarize the blog.** They stand alone as valuable content.
- **The blog link is the CTA, not the content.** If someone reads only the LinkedIn post, they still got value.
- **Bob's posts are personal.** First person, story-driven, opinion-forward. Never sounds like a press release.
- **DS posts are generous.** Framework-forward, data-driven. Sounds like a smart colleague sharing knowledge.
- **Post timing:** Bob's post within 24 hours of blog publish. DS post 2-3 days after.

### Example: Week 4 Blog → LinkedIn Extraction

**Blog:** "How CommuniCare recovered $10M in PDPM revenue with one integration"

**Extraction 1 → Bob's LinkedIn (Story Snippet):**
"The first week of our CommuniCare engagement, we didn't write a single line of code. We sat in on MDS assessments. Watched nurses toggle between 4 screens to complete documentation that should take 15 minutes but took 45. The $10M revenue recovery started there — not with AI, but with understanding the workflow that was broken."

**Extraction 2 → DS LinkedIn (Case Proof):**
"$10M recovered in PDPM revenue. One PointClickCare integration. Here's what made it work:
- Discovery before development (3 weeks understanding the MDS workflow)
- AI validation against real facility data (not vendor benchmarks)
- Continuous optimization post-launch (month 8 ROI > month 1)
Read the full story: [link]"

## 10. Storytelling Framework

### The DS METHOD Narrative

Every piece of content maps back to one arc:

```
THE WORLD SAYS:  Buy a product, deploy it, hope it works.
WE SAY:          Understand your data first, test with evidence,
                 build for production, then optimize continuously.
THE RESULT:      Healthcare organizations that compound their ROI
                 instead of replacing their vendors.
```

### Three Narrative Modes

| Mode | Voice | When to Use | Frequency |
|------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Origin Story** | Bob, first person | Why DS exists. The founding insight. Deeply personal. | 1-2x per quarter |
| **Client Story** | DS voice, third person | A specific organization's journey through the METHOD. The client is the hero; DS is the guide. | 1-2x per month (Permission to Build pillar) |
| **Expert Story** | Either voice | A domain-specific insight demonstrating depth. "Here's what we've learned from 50+ healthcare AI implementations." | Weekly (Playbook pillar, blog posts) |

### The METHOD as Story Structure

Each METHOD phase is a narrative beat:

| Phase | Story Beat | Emotional Arc | Content Example |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Discover** | "We listened." | Empathy, validation | Week 4: first week at CommuniCare |
| **Experiment** | "We tested." | Rigor, courage to say no | Week 7: POC playbook |
| **Engineer** | "We built." | Confidence, production reality | Week 8: EHR integration |
| **Optimize** | "We compounded." | Patience, long-term thinking | Week 11: year 2 ROI |

### Voice Do's and Don'ts

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "We embed with your team" | "We are the premier healthcare AI partner" |
| "$10M recovered in PDPM revenue" | "Significant revenue improvements" |
| "Off-the-shelf AI doesn't know your business" | "We offer customized solutions" |
| "No pitch. Just an honest conversation." | "Schedule a demo today!" |
| "You can do this. Here's how." | "Only we can solve this problem." |
| "Here's what broke (and how we fixed it)" | "Our seamless implementation process" |
